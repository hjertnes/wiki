The Power of the internal access level

  • Notes
  • Development
  • [[C#]]

C# and .NET have a access keyword called "internal". In .NET you typically have a solution with multiple projects, typically at least a main one and a unit test one. But sometimes more. The internal keyword makes something availble within that project (or nuget package) plus any other assembly you whitelist.

This is a very useful way to restrict anything you don't want anyone else to use.
